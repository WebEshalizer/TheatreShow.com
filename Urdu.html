1. Jashn-e-Rekhta

Jashn-e-Rekhta (Urdu: جشنِ ریختہ, lit. 'Celebration of Rekhta') is the world's largest Urdu language literary festival. It is a three-day event held in New Delhi that celebrates the Urdu language. This event is flagship event of rekhta foundation.

History. Rekhta was launched on 11 January 2013 in New Delhi, India. The portal came into existence after the idea of "Urdu virtual library" was introduced by Sanjiv Saraf to professors of Urdu from across leading universities.

The root word being “Rekhtan” in Persian which means to break up or Scatter

2. Heer Ranjha

Heer Ranjha is part of the Qissa genre of tragic love stories, along with tales such as Laila Majnu and Sassui Punnhun. Because its plot involves a romance opposed by family members and ends with the two lovers dying, the story is often compared to the Shakespeare play Romeo and Juliet.

Heer Ranjha is a famous sad, love story from the Punjab . Other Punjabi love stories are Mirza Sahiban, and Sohni Mahiwal. This story has been written by different writers, at different times. The best and most famous is Heer by the poet Waris Shah written in 1761.

4. Shab Bakhir Maa

On the 24th day of the ongoing “Pakistan Theatre Festival” in Arts Council of Pakistan Karachi, the Theatre play “Shab Bakhir Maa” was presented in Auditorium I, written by Marsha Norman and directed by Samina Nazir, Marsha Norman is an American playwright, screenwriter and novelist While the cast of the drama included Manal Siddiqui and Rachna Kirpalani, drama takes the audience on a journey into the essence of existence where the fundamental dilemma of choosing between existence and oblivion takes center stage.

The story of the play revolves around a mother and daughter. In the play, a daughter named Sarah, fed up with her failed marriage, illness and society’s attitudes, decides to commit suicide. War breaks out, war breaks out between mother and daughter when daughter sarah decided to commit suicide, this drama takes you on a deep exploration, the drama goes out to unravel the complex layers of the meaning of life, the realistic performances of Manal Siddiqui and Rachana kirpalani in the Theatre highly appreciated by audience.

6. Taj Mahal Ka Tender

The original play ‘Taj Mahal Ka Tender’ is a sharp satire that puts the Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan’s efforts to build the wonder of the world Taj Mahal, in the context of today’s time and date. How the current state of society, burdened with archaic government laws, corruption and red tapism would impact such a project is expressed using biting dark humor. Originally written in Hindi by Ajay Shukla, Taj Mahal Ka Tender has a strong grip over its spectators since decades and has won prizes like Sahitya Kala Parishad and Mohan Rakesh Samman. The students of Rangayan, who performed the play on evening of 24th of October 2016 , adapted the original and experimented with certain parts to add a contemporary punch.
